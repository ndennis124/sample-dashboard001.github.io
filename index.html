Evidence-Based Principles

📈
Progressive Overload
Add 2.5–5 lbs to upper body and 5–10 lbs to lower body lifts when you hit the top of a rep range for 2 consecutive sessions. This is the #1 driver of hypertrophy.
🔄
Rep Range & Load
ACSM recommends 8–12 reps at 70–85% of 1RM for hypertrophy. Compound lifts stay in 6–8 range; isolation work goes 12–15. Both ranges drive growth.
⏸️
Rest Intervals
90 sec–3 min between compound sets; 60–90 sec for isolations. Longer rests preserve strength output and drive more total volume per session.
⚠️
Proximity to Failure
Leave 1–3 reps in reserve (RIR) on most sets. Train to true failure sparingly — it accelerates fatigue and risks injury without adding much extra stimulus.
🌙
Deload Every 4–6 Weeks
Cut volume by 40–50% for one week. Reduces accumulated fatigue and often results in strength PRs the week after. Non-negotiable for longevity.
🧗
Climbing Integration
Monthly climbing is excellent functional pulling volume — lats, biceps, forearms, core. On climbing weeks, skip or shorten Monday's pull session and reduce Tuesday entirely.
